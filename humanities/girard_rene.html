Introduction

René Girard (1923-2015) was a French literary critic and philosopher whose work is not always easy to categorise. In addition to literature and philosophy, his work is also relevant philosophical anthropology, psychology and economics (although he did not publish in all these areas). His work is based on the analysis of literary and religious texts, not on actual research in psychology or anthropology (i.e. no psychological experiments, ethnohistoric research or ethnographic research)

After writing a PhD dissertation in history, he moved from France to the United States, where his first journal articles focused on French literature. In his first book, Mensonge romantique et vérité romanesque (1961), he first formulated his theory about mimetic desire, based on an analysis of novels by Cervantes, Flaubert, Stenhal, Proust and Dostoyevski. His second major work, La Violence et le sacré (1972) is the result of thinking through the consequences of mimesis for early human societies. This led to the formulation of his theory about the role of the scapegoat mechanism in the emergence of human culture and religion. His 1978 book Des choses cachées depuis la fondation du monde (1978) continued his work in this direction and added his theory that the Bible reveals and denounces the scapegoat mechanism. Several other books followed, including La route antique des hommes pervers (1985), which focuses on the book of Job, and A Theater of Envy: William Shakespeare (1991), in which he shows that Shakespeare was aware of the mechanisms of mimesis and scapegoating.

Websites

  • The Colloquium on Violence & Religion (COV&R): this is an international association founded in 1990 that publishes the yearly journal Contagion and a bulletin (for members), and that organises a yearly conference devoted to the ramifications of Girard's mimetic theory for theology, philosophical anthropology, psychology and literary criticism.
  • Imitatio is a project of the Thiel Foundation that furthers research and the application of Girar's mimetic theory across the social sciences. Peter Thiel was influenced by the mimetic theory (see this interview on YouTube). See also Imitatio's YouTube channel.
  • The Raven Foundation (established in 2007) is committed to making religion reasonable, violence unthinkable and peace a possibility by spreading awareness of the transformative power of mimetic theory. See also the Raven Foundation's YouTube channel.
  • Association Recherches Mimétiques (established in 2006) is a French organisation that structure and make widely known the research inspired by René Girard's mimetic theory. It is the French branch of Imitatio. The organisation also has a YouTube channel.
  • Girard Studiekring: the Dutch Girard Society, which started meeting in the 1980s. Its entire website is in Dutch.
  • Site Violence et Sacré, French website by Jean-Paul Kornobis. An English version of the home page is also available, but the rest of the site has not been translated.
  • René Girard, French website by Philippe Cottet.
  • Anthropoetics: The Journal of Generative Anthropology is an Open Access journal that took is starting point from Girard's theory about the origin of human culture. Eric Gans, who started the journal, also wrote A Brief Introduction to Generative Anthropology.

Books

  • Girard, René: Mensonge romantique et vérité romanesque. Grasset, 1961. ISBN 9782246004073.
    Girard's first book analyses novels by Cervantes, Flaubert, Stendahl and Dostoyevsky, and formulates his theory of mimetic desire.
  • Girard, René: Dostoïevski : du double à l’unité. Gérard Monfort, 1963.
    An analysis of several of Dostoyevsky's novels, starting with Notes from Underground. This essay was later reprinted in Critique dans un souterrain (see below).
  • Girard, René: La Violence et le sacré. Grasset, 1972. ISBN 9782246000518.
    In his third book, Girard looks for mimesis in myths and older literary texts (especially Greek tragedy) and describes how mimesis leads to scapegoating and the origin of human culture and religion.
  • Girard, René: Critique dans un souterrain. L'Âge d'Homme, 1976.
    Contains his earlier text on Dostoyevsky, a discussion of Albert Camus and a critique of Gilles Deleuze and Félix Guattari's L'anti-Oedipe.
  • Girard, René: To Double Business Bound: Essays on Literature, Mimesis, and Anthropology. Johns Hopkins University Press, 1978.
    A collection of nine articles and an interview.
  • Girard, René: Des choses cachées depuis la fondation du monde: Recherches avec Jean-Michel Oughourlian et Guy Lefort. Grasset, 1978. ISBN 9782246618416.
    This book consists of conversations between René Girard and the psychiatrists Jean-Michel Oughourlian and Guy Lefort. Girard clarifies certain parts of La Violence et le sacré and formulates his theory about the New Testament as a book that unveils and denounces scapegoating mechanisms.
    Translations:
    • Things Hidden Since the Foundation of the World, translated by Stephen Bann and Michael Metteer. Stanford University Press, 1987. ISBN 9780804722155.
  • Girard, René: Le Bouc émissaire. Grasset, 1982. ISBN 9782246267812.
    Much of this book discusses the persecution of victims or scapegoats in medieval texts. According to Philippe Cottet, this book should not be read as an introduction to Girard's work.
  • Girard, René: La Route antique des hommes pervers. Grasset, 1985. ISBN 9782246351115.
    About the book of Job.
    Translations:
    • Job: The Victim of His People. Translated by Yvonne Freccero. Stanford University Press, 1987. ISBN 9780804714044.
  • Hamerton-Kelly, Robert, ed.: Violent Origins: Walter Burkert, René Girard, and Jonathan Z. Smith on Ritual Killing and Cultural Formation. Stanford University Press, 1987. ISBN 9780804715188.
    Translations:
    • Girard, René: Sanglantes origines. Entretiens avec Walter Burkert, Renato Rosaldo et Jonathan Z. Smith. Flammarion, 2011, 2013. ISBN 9782081289529.
  • Girard, René: A Theater of Envy: Shakespeare Oxford University Press, 1991.
    French translation: Shakespeare : les feux de l'envie. Grasset, 1990. ISBN 9782246249917.
    This book is based on journal articles that Girard had published in the 1970s and 1980s. Girard shows that Shakespeare understood mimetic desire, even in early works such as The Two Gentlemen of Verona and The Rape of Lucrece. Later works also show his understanding of collective violence and scapegoating. However, according to Girard, Shakespeare chose to hint at these behavioural mechanisms in subtext rather than discussing them openly.
  • Girard, René: Quand ces choses commenceront …. Arléa, 1996. ISBN 9782869593008.
    A book of interviews with Michel Treguer.
  • Girard, René: Je vois Satan tomber comme l'éclair. Grasset, 1999. ISBN 9782246267911.
  • Girard, René: Celui par qui le scandale arrive. Desclée de Brouwer, 2001.
  • Girard, René: La voix méconnue du réel. Grasset, 2002. ISBN 9782246611011.

    English translation: Sacrifice. Translated by Matthew Patillo and David Dawson. Breakthroughs in Mimetic Theory Series. Michigan State University Press, 2011. ISBN 978-0-87013-992-5.

    This is a collection of 20 essays, seven of which were originally written in French. The essays cover French authors such as Saint-John Perse, Valéry, Stendhal, Simone de Beauvoir, Marcel Proust, Jean Racine, Victor Hugo and Chrétien de Troyes; it also contains essays on Dostoyevsky and Shakespeare.
