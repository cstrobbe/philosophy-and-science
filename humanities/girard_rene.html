Introduction

René Girard (1923-2015) was a French literary critic and philosopher whose work is not always easy to categorise. In addition to literature and philosophy, his work is also relevant philosophical anthropology, psychology and economics (although he did not publish in all these areas). His work is based on the analysis of literary and religious texts, not on actual research in psychology or anthropology (i.e. no field research).

After writing a PhD dissertation in history, he moved from France to the United States, where his first journal articles focused on French literature. In his first book, Mensonge romantique et vérité romanesque (1961), he first formulated his theory about mimetic desire, based on an analysis of novels by Cervantes, Flaubert, Stenhal, Proust and Dostoyevski. His second major work, La Violence et le sacré (1972) is the consequence of thinking through the consequences of mimesis for early human societies. This led to the formulation of his theory about the role of the scapegoat mechanism in the emergence of human culture and religion. His 1978 book Des choses cachées depuis la fondation du monde (1978) continued his work in this direction and added his theory that the Bible reveals and denounces the scapegoat mechanism. Several other books followed, including La route antique des hommes pervers (1985), which focuses on the book of Job, and A Theatre of Envy: William Shakespeare (1991), in which he shows that Shakespeare was aware of the mechanisms of mimesis and scapegoating.

Websites

  • The Colloquium on Violence & Religion (COV&R): this is an international association founded in 1990 that publishes the yearly journal Contagion and a bulletin (for members), and that organises a yearly conference devoted to the ramifications of Girard's mimetic theory for theology, philosophical anthropology, psychology and literary criticism.
  • Imitatio is a project of the Thiel Foundation that furthers research and the application of Girar's mimetic theory across the social sciences. Peter Thiel was influenced by the mimetic theory (see this interview on YouTube). See also Imitatio's YouTube channel.
  • The Raven Foundation (established in 2007) is committed to making religion reasonable, violence unthinkable and peace a possibility by spreading awareness of the transformative power of mimetic theory. See also the Raven Foundation's YouTube channel.
  • Association Recherches Mimétiques (established in 2006) is a French organisation that structure and make widely known the research inspired by René Girard's mimetic theory. It is the French branch of Imitatio. The organisation also has a YouTube channel.
  • Girard Studiekring: the Dutch Girard Society, which started meeting in the 1980s. Its entire website is in Dutch.
  • Anthropoetics: The Journal of Generative Anthropology is an Open Access journal that took is starting point from Girar's theory about the origin of human culture. Eric Gans, who started the journal, also wrote A Brief Introduction to Generative Anthropology.
