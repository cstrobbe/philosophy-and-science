Die Philosophen haben die Welt nur verschieden interpretiert; es kömmt drauf an, sie zu verändern. Karl Marx, Thesen über Feuerbach, 1845.

Karl Marx (1818 – 1883) was one of the most influential philosophers and thinkers of the nineteenth century.

Introductions, Biographies and Overviews

The English Wikipedia article Karl Marx and the German Wikipedia article Karl Marx contain a basic bibliography.

Works

Marx's works are now in the public domain, so several web sites provide free access to many of his texts. For example, Wikisource (German), Wikisource (English), Project Gutenberg (Project Gutenberg blocks access from Germany as of February 2018), Zeno.org and the Internet Archive. The German Wikipedia article Werkverzeichnis claims to provide a complete list.

Books on Marx or Marxism

Titles are listed in order of publication.

  • Fromm, Erich: Marx's Concept of Man. 1961. See the Wikipedia article Marx's Concept of Man.
  • Althusser, Louis; Balibar, Étienne; Establet, Roger; Macherey, Pierre; Rancière, Jacques: Lire le Capital. 1965. See the Wikipedia article Lire le Capital.
  • Lukes, Steven: Marxism and Morality. 1985. See the Wikipedia article Marxism and Morality.
  • Davis, Mike: Old Gods, New Enigmas: Marx’s Lost Theory. Verso, 2018 (320 pages) ISBN 9781788732161.
    From the publisher's description: Marx has returned, but which Marx? Recent biographies have proclaimed him to be an emphatically nineteenth-century figure, but in this book, Mike Davis’s first directly about Marx and Marxism, a thinker comes to light who speaks to the present as much as the past.
  • Claeys, Gregory: Marx and Marxism. Penguin, 2018. (544 pages) ISBN 9780141983486.
    From the publisher's book description (…) In this wide-ranging account, Gregory Claeys, one of Britain's leading historians of socialism, considers Marx's ideas and their development through the Russian Revolution to the present, showing why Marx and Marxism still matter today.
  • Heinrich, Michael: Kritik der politischen Ökonomie. Eine Einführung in «Das Kapital» von Karl Marx. 14. Auflage. Schmetterling Verlag, 2018. ISBN 3-89657-697-6.
  • Eagleton, Terry: Why Marx Was Right. Yale University Press, 2018. (280 pages) ISBN 9780300231069.
  • Fusaro, Diago: Schon wieder Marx. Die Wiederkehr der Revolution. Übersetzt von Jenny Perelli. Westend Verlag, 2018. (96 pages) ISBN 9783864891816.
    See also Fusaro's article Die Rückkehr eines Verdrängten.
  • Wolff, Richard D.: Understanding Marxism. Democracy at Work, 2018. ISBN 978-0-359-46702-0.
    From the publisher's book description: (…) There are rising demands for accessible introductions to Marxism and to the social changes it suggests. This essay responds to that interest. It seeks to provide bases for real solutions now that the flaws and failures of contemporary capitalism are exposed again, and again. Marxism always was the critical shadow of capitalism. Their interactions changed them both. Now Marxism is once again stepping into the light as capitalism shakes from its own excesses and confronts decline. Hopefully this book can help our era’s renewal of Marxism. (…)
  • Understanding Marxism: Q&A with Richard D. Wolff (Democracy at Work on YouTube, 114 minutes, 13.06.2019).
    Prof. Wolff talks about the impetus for the book and why Marxism is appealing to a growing audience. Recorded at Judson Memorial Church on 12.06.2019.
