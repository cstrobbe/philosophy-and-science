Introductory Lectures (YouTube)

  • Paul Hoyningen-Huene is a German philosopher who has published several of his lectures on philosophy of science on YouTube:
    • Philosophy of Science: a series of lectures taught in 2010 at the Leibniz Universität Hannover (mostly to students of horticulture and students of water resources and environmental management; 32 videos, varying in length between 20 and 90 minutes).
    • Philosophy of Science: Inductivism, Popper, Kuhn (10 videos)
    • Einführung in Erkenntnis- und Wissenschaftstheorie: a course in German taught at the Leibniz Universität Hannover in 2013-2014 (5 videos).
  • Hilary Putnam on “The Philosophy of Science”: part 1 (10 minutes), part 2 (11 minutes), part 3 (11 minutes), part 4 (6 minutes) & part 5 (8 minutes). These videos are from a series of interviews conducted by Bryan Magee that were broadcast by the BBC in the 1980s.
  • Wissenschaftstheorie is a series of lectures taught by Torsten Wilholt at the Leibniz Universität Hannover. (45 videos)
  • Philosophie des Sciences, a series of lectures taught at CentraleSupélec in 2012 by the French physicist Étienne Klein. (9 videos, on average 15 minutes long).

Introductory Books

There are many introductions to philosophy of science on the market. Some are very short (e.g. the books by Tetens, Okasha and Lecourt) and primarily aimed at the general public, while others are aimed at students (e.g. Chalmers, Wiltsche and Schülein).

  • Tetens, Holm: Wissenschaftstheorie: Eine Einführung. C. H. Beck, 2013. ISBN 978-3-406-65331-5.
  • Okasha, Samir: Philosophy of Science: A Very Short Introduction. Oxford University Press, 2002. ISBN 978-0-19-280283-5.
  • Lecourt, Dominique: La philosophie des sciences. Que sais-je? 3624. 5e édition. Paris: Presses Universitaires de France, 2013. ISBN 978-2-13-058053-9.
  • Eberhard, Kurt: Einführung in die Erkenntnis- und Wissenschaftstheorie. Urban-Taschenbücher 386. Stuttgart: Kohlhammer, 1987.
  • Chalmers, Alan F.: What Is This Thing Called Science? Fourth edition. Indianapolis/Cambridge: Hackett Publishing, 2013 (282 p.) ISBN 978-1-62466-038-2. McGrawHill, 2013 (316 p.) ISBN 978 03 35 26 27 86.
  • Wiltsche, Harald A.: Einführung in die Wissenschaftstheorie. UTB 3936. Vandenhoeck & Ruprecht, 2013. ISBN 978 38 25 23 93 67.
  • Lewens, Tim: The Meaning of Science: A Pelican Introduction. Pelican, 2015. ISBN 978-0-141-97742-3.

Criticism of Science and Scientism

  • Paul Feyerabend - How to Defend Society Against Science is part of a course on Social and Political Theory by Nicole Pepperell (RMIT University).
  • Rupert Sheldrake: The Science Delusion, a talk given at TEDxWhitechapel in January 2013. This talk is based on ideas expressed in Sheldrake's book The Science Delusion and caused controversy and the video was taken down from the TED website.
