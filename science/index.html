History of Science

  • Vermij, Rienk: Kleine geschiedenis van de wetenschap. Nieuwezijds, 2005. ISBN 9789057122248 (278 p.).
    A very readable introduction that focuses on how the world view of (originally) philosophers and (later) scientists evolved and how this changing perspective both gave rise to new methods and was influence by these methods. In addition to the evolution of these methods and theories, the book also discusses the importance of scientific instruments, laboratories and journals.

Registering All Medical Trials

The British physician Ben Goldacre founded the AllTrials campaign to require open science practices in clinical trials. See www.alltrials.net. The AllTrials website explains why this initiative matters. The main reason is that trials with negative results are twice as likely to remain unreported as those with positive results so doctors and other people who make decisions about medicines don’t have full information about the benefits and risks of treatments we use every day. For this reason, each clinical trial should be registered before it starts. One of the benefits is that it should be easier to find out whether research results are being withheld. Anyone can sign the petition to make a registry of all trials a reality.

HdM Science Day

Each semester, Stuttgart Media University (Hochschule der Medien) organises a “science day”. This is an event where researches present topics that are not part of the regular curriculum at the university. The presentations can also be viewed through a live video stream; the videos recordings are then archived.
