Each semester, Stuttgart Media University (Hochschule der Medien) organises a “science day”. This is an event where researches present topics that are not part of the regular curriculum at the university. The presentations can also be viewed through a live video stream; the videos recordings are then archived.

The Kangxi Emperor

The Kangxi Emperor's reign (1661 - 1772, 61 years) was the longest in Chinese history. The Kangxi Emperor is considered one of China's greatest emperors. One of the cultural achievements of his reign was the compilation of the Kangxi Dictionary. The Flemish Jesuit missionary Ferdinand Verbiest was appointed Head of the Mathematical Board and Director of the Observatory during the Kangxi Emperor's reign.

  • Jami, Catherine: The Emperor's New Mathematics: Western Learning and Imperial Authority During the Kangxi Reign (1662-1722). Oxford University Press, 2011. (452 pages) ISBN 9780199601400.
  • Spence, Jonathan: Emperor of China: Self-Portrait of K’ang-Hsi. Vintage, 1988. (256 pages) ISBN 9780679720744.

Myths about the Middle Ages

  • People thought that the Earth was flat; see Flat earth (Wikipedia). (It is actually hard to find Medieval claims that the Earth was flat.)
  • Witch burning was common in the Middle Ages (see e.g. the witch hunting scene in Monty Python and the Holy Grail). Witch hunting was more common in the late Middle Ages and the Renaissance. The infamous Malleus Maleficarum / Hexenhammer was probably first published in 1486, i.e. at the end of the Middle Ages. See also witch-hunt (Wikipedia), Hexenverfolgung (Wikipedia).
  • Droit du seigneur aka ius primae noctis: droit du seigneur (Wikipedia).
    See also:
    • Boureau, Alain: The Lord's First Night: The Myth of the Droit de Cuissage. Translated by Lydia G. Cochrane. University of Chicago Press, 1998. ISBN 9780226067438.
  • Chastity belts: chastity belt (Wikipedia).
    See also:
    • Laskow, Sarah: Everything You've Heard About Chastity Belts Is a Lie, Atlas Obscura, 17 August 2015.
  • Religious taboos prevented medical dissection of bodies. See Katharine Park’s book about late medieval dissection. See also Secrets of Women: Gender, Generation, and the Origins of Human Dissection. Zone Books, 2006. ISBN 9781890951672.
  • Vikings had horned helmets. (Debunked on Wikipedia.)
  • James Franklin: Myths about the Middle Ages.
  • Pernoud, Régine: Those Terrible Middle Ages: Debunking the Myths. Ignatius Press, 2000. ISBN 0898707811.
    Pernoud, Régine: Pour en finir avec le Moyen Age. [1944] Points, 2014. ISBN 978-2757841075.
  • (Potentially relevant: ) Thomas, Keith: Religion and the Decline of Magic. Peregrine Books, 1978.
