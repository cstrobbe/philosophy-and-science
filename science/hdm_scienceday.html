Each semester, Stuttgart Media University (Hochschule der Medien) organises a “science day”. This is an event where researches present topics that are not part of the regular curriculum at the university. The presentations can also be viewed through a live video stream; the videos recordings are then archived.

European History

The Crusades Through Arab / Islamic Eyes

The Crusades are one of many topics that Westerners only learn about from a Western point of view. Since the 1980s (and possibly even earlier), there have been several publications that also present the Islamic point of view.

  • Maalouf, Amin: Les Croisades vues par les Arabes. J’ai lu, 1999, ISBN 2290119164. / JC Lattès, 1983/1986, ISBN 2-7096-0547-3.
  • Cobb, Paul M.: The Race for Paradise: An Islamic History of the Crusades. Oxford University Press, 2014. ISBN 978-0-19-953201-8.
  • Tyerman, Christopher: The Crusades: A Very Short Introduction. Oxford University Press, 2005. ISBN 978-0-19-280655-0.
  • Riley-Smith, Jonathan, ed.: The Oxford History of the Crusades. Oxford University Press, 2002. ISBN 978-0-19-280312-2.

Myths about the Middle Ages

  • People thought that the Earth was flat; see Flat earth (Wikipedia). (It is actually hard to find Medieval claims that the Earth was flat.)
  • Witch burning was common in the Middle Ages (see e.g. the witch hunting scene in Monty Python and the Holy Grail). Witch hunting was more common in the late Middle Ages and the Renaissance. The infamous Malleus Maleficarum / Hexenhammer was probably first published in 1486, i.e. at the end of the Middle Ages. See also witch-hunt (Wikipedia), Hexenverfolgung (Wikipedia).
  • Droit du seigneur aka ius primae noctis: droit du seigneur (Wikipedia).
    See also:
    • Boureau, Alain: The Lord's First Night: The Myth of the Droit de Cuissage. Translated by Lydia G. Cochrane. University of Chicago Press, 1998. ISBN 9780226067438.
  • Chastity belts: chastity belt (Wikipedia).
    See also:
    • Laskow, Sarah: Everything You've Heard About Chastity Belts Is a Lie, Atlas Obscura, 17 August 2015.
  • Religious taboos prevented medical dissection of bodies. See Katharine Park’s book about late medieval dissection. See also Secrets of Women: Gender, Generation, and the Origins of Human Dissection. Zone Books, 2006. ISBN 9781890951672.
  • Vikings had horned helmets. (Debunked on Wikipedia.)
  • James Franklin: Myths about the Middle Ages.
  • Pernoud, Régine: Those Terrible Middle Ages: Debunking the Myths. Ignatius Press, 2000. ISBN 0898707811.
    Pernoud, Régine: Pour en finir avec le Moyen Age. [1944] Points, 2014. ISBN 978-2757841075.

The Scottish Enlightenment

The Enlightenment is strongly associated with French thinkers such as Voltaire, Rouseau and Diderot, and with the German philosopher Immanuel Kant. The Scottish Enlightenment is less well known in continental Europe.

  • Wikipedia: Scottish Enlightenment.
  • Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy: Scottish Philosophy in the 18th Century.
  • Encyclopædia Britannica: Scottish Enlightenment.
  • BBC: The Scottish Enlightenment – mid 18th century.
  • Disability History Scotland: The Scottish Enlightenment.
    Alexander Broadie: Scottish Enlightenment (Birlinn, 2007)<
  • Broadie, Alexander: The Cambridge Companion to the Scottish Enlightenment. Cambridge University Press, 2003.
  • Sher, Richard B.: The Enlightenment and the Book: Scottish Authors and Their Publishers in Eighteenth-Century Britain, Ireland, and America. University of Chicago Press, 2007.
  • Ahnert, Thomas; Manning, Susan: haracter, Self, and Sociability in the Scottish Enlightenment. Palgrave Studies in Cultural and Intellectual History. 2011.
  • Wilson, David B.: Seeking Nature's Logic: Natural Philosophy in the Scottish Enlightenment. Pennsylvania State University Press, 2009.
