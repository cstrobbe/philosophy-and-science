Books and Articles on Doctoral Research and Thesis Writing

  • Gustavii, Björn: How to Prepare a Scientific Doctoral Dissertation Based on Research Articles. Cambridge University Press, 2012. (101 pages) ISBN 9781107669048.
  • Evans, David; Gruba, Paul; Zobel, Justin: How to Write a Better Thesis. Springer, 2014. (167 pages) 978-3-319-04285-5 (paperback) / ISBN 978-3-319-04286-2 (e-book).
  • Gosling, Patricia; Noordam, Lambertus D.: Mastering Your PhD: Survival and Success in the Doctoral Years and Beyond. Second edition. ISBN 978-3-642-15846-9 (paperback) / Springer, 2011. (240 pages) ISBN 978-3-642-15847-6 (e-book).
  • Petre, Marian; Rugg, Gordon: The Unwritten Rules of PhD Research. Open University Press, 2010. (230 pages) ISBN 9780335237029 (paper)/ ISBN 9780335240265 (e-book).
    Book review by Caroline Brennan (2011).
  • Mullins, Gerry; Kiley, Margaret: 'It's a PhD, not a Nobel Prize': How experienced examiners assess research theses, Studies in Higher Education, Volume 27, Issue 4 (2002): 369-386.
  • Holbrook, Allyson; Bourke, Sid; Fairbairn, Hedy; Lovat, Terry: Examiner comment on the literature review in Ph.D. theses, Studies in Higher Education, Volume 32, Issue 3 (2007): 337-356. (The full paper is available on ResearchGate.)
  • Golding, Clinton; Sharmini, Sharon; Lazarovitch, Ayelet: What examiners do: what thesis students should know, Assessment & Evaluation in Higher Education, Volume 39, Issue 5 (2014): 563-576 (Open Access).
  • Kennedy, Ian Geoffrey: How to do Research: Today's Tips and Tools. 2016. ISBN 978-0-620-71929-2. Self-published e-book for Amazon Kindle. Part H of the book covers writing.
